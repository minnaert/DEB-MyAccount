Direct Energy Business Daily Market Update October 14, 2015

Early Morning Update

The Nov15 natural gas contract is trading down $.01 at $2.49. The Nov15 crude contract is down $0.06 at $46.60.

Summary

After five straight sessions of gains based mostly on technical trading, Nov15 natural gas futures broke below $2.50/MMBtu yesterday, as traders returned to selling amidst a lack of fundamental support. The prompt month traded as high as $2.555/MMBtu, before closing Tuesday at $2.498/MMBtu, down 3.7 cents. The downside is being driven by the latest weather outlooks, which have turned much milder than previous forecasts. Cooler-than-normal temperatures should clear out of the eastern half of the U.S. over the next ten days, while the western half of the country continues to experience above-average temperatures, with the West Coast five to eight degrees above normal. The moderate temperatures should inhibit demand for natural gas for power generation, as well as space heating. The declining demand should also boost the final weeks of the storage injection season, which is currently on track to finish at the highest level ever before the onset of winter.

Bullish Factors
  • Exports to Mexico at record high
  • Gas prices near production cost levels
  • Growing gas demand for power generation
Bearish Factors
  • Mild weather, low demand
  • Strong NG production
  • 5YA storage surplus
