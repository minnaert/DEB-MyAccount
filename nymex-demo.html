What are NYMEX Alerts?

Energy commodities are traded on the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX) and "NYMEX Alerts" available on the My Account portal of www.business.directenergy.com will allow customers to monitor price movements in Henry Hub natural gas along with several regional power indices and be alerted when the commodity price rises above or falls below specified levels. NYMEX Alerts are a valuable tool for customers who want to track commodity price trends in a time efficient manner. When a NYMEX Alert is set and if a NYMEX market price exceeds the high set price or goes below the low set price, customers will receive emails and messages in the My Account Message Center. NYMEX Alerts can be used to notify customers of changes in market trends or risks or opportunities in the execution of their risk management plan.

Creating new NYMEX Alerts

After logging in to My Account, a customer can find the NYMEX Alerts they have created in the NYMEX Alerts section in Preferences. Above the list of currently active NYMEX alerts, a customer can then select New Alert to monitor a new commodity.

Users can create as many NYMEX Alerts as they wish. Multiple alerts can be created for the same commodity or delivery date.

The first step is to name the alert, choose a commodity and choose a delivery date up to four years in the future.

There are many commodities available to create NYMEX alerts for. Natural Gas has a single commodity market to monitor, whereas the power markets are regional.

Once the commodity and delivery date are selected, the most recently NYMEX price is displayed (please note that the price feed utilizes delayed quotes and will show the "last traded price" which could be as recent as 20-30 minutes old or date back to the prior day's closing price - depending on the commodity, time of day and delivery month). Next, high and low values must be entered. My Account will only allow high values that are above the displayed NYMEX price and the low values that are below the NYMEX price. To complete the process, save the alert.

The delivery month is populated based on the selected commodity. Up to 4 years of future delivery prices may be available.

My Account will only allow high values that are above the displayed NYMEX price and the low values that are below the NYMEX price. The most recently NYMEX price is displayed, based on the selected commodity and delivery date above the high and low values. When creating or editing a NYMEX alert, the high and low values are validated to ensure appropriate values are entered.

Working With Your Active NYMEX Alerts

The NYMEX Alerts section in Preferences displays the alerts the user has created. Active alerts are those that have not reached the Delivery date (expiration of the specified NYMEX futures contract). Once the delivery date has been reached, an alert becomes expired. Expired NYMEX alerts are listed in the Expired NYMEX Alerts lists.

From the Active NYMEX alerts section, you can edit or delete any alert you have created.

Active NYMEX Alerts can be edited and deleted. Expired NYMEX alerts can be deleted, but not edited.

The Active NYMEX alerts section displays the alerts you have created which have not reached their delivery date (expiration of the specified NYMEX futures contract). These active alerts will generate messages and emails when their thresholds are met. Once the delivery date has been reached, the "Expired NYMEX Alerts" section will be displayed.

NYMEX alerts are individual to each user in My Account.  When a NYMEX Alert is set and if a NYMEX market price exceeds the high set price or goes below the low set price, the alert messages will be sent to the single email address for the user who created the alert. If you have other members on your team who have access to your Direct Energy accounts, they will need to create their own alerts.

Editing Active Alerts

Any value of a NYMEX alert can be edited. After the changes are saved, the alert will use the updated information to generate alerts. An email and message will be sent to the user with the details of the edited values of the alert.

Any value of an alert can be edited. The same rules for ensuring valid values are entered are still enforced when editing an alert.

NYMEX Alert Emails

When a NYMEX Alert is set and if a NYMEX market price exceeds the high set price or goes below the low set price, customers will receive an email from My Account.

When you create or edit a NYMEX alert, a message is sent to the email address for the user who created the NYMEX alert.

NYMEX Alert Message Center

When a NYMEX Alert is set and if a NYMEX market price exceeds the high set price or goes below the low set price, customers will receive emails and messages in the My Account Message Center. The My Account Message Center will display many different types of communications from Direct Energy Business, including NYMEX related messages.

When you create or edit a NYMEX alert, a message is sent to your My Account Message Center.

Working With Your Expired NYMEX Alerts

The NYMEX Alerts section in Preferences displays all of the alerts the user has created. Those alerts which have reached their delivery date (expiration of the specified NYMEX futures contract) are displayed in the Expired Alerts section. This section is collapsed by default. If there are no expired alerts to display, this section is not shown. Expired alerts will not generate messages and emails. Expired alerts can be deleted, but not edited.

Expired NYMEX alerts can be deleted, but not edited.

Creating Strip and Weighted Average Strip Alerts

Creating the equivalent of strip pricing.

A strip price is the average of the daily settlement prices for a multiple month range of futures contracts, and can be a good indicator of where energy commodity prices are for those months. Some customers may find this type of pricing helpful as a part of their risk management plan.

Creating the equivalent of weighted average strip pricing.

Weighted average strip pricing is an additional calculation for monitoring prices. A weighted average gives different weights to various prices included.
