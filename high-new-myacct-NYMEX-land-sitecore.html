What are Energy Market Alerts?

Energy commodities are traded on the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX) and "Energy Market Alerts" (formerly known as "NYMEX Alerts") available on the My Account portal of www.business.directenergy.com will allow customers to monitor price movements in Henry Hub natural gas along with several regional power indices and be alerted when the commodity price rises above or falls below specified levels. Energy Market Alerts are a valuable tool for customers who want to track commodity price trends in a time efficient manner. When a Energy Market Alert is set and if a market price exceeds the high set price or goes below the low set price, customers will receive emails and messages in the My Account Message Center. Energy Market Alerts can be used to notify customers of changes in market trends or risks or opportunities in the execution of their risk management plan.

Creating new Energy Market Alerts

After logging in to My Account, a customer can find the Energy Market Alerts they have created in the Energy Market Alerts section in Preferences. Above the list of currently active Energy Market alerts, a customer can then select New Alert to monitor a new energy market.

Users can create as many Energy Market Alerts as they wish. Multiple alerts can be created for the same energy market or delivery date.

There are 3 types of alerts which can be created in My Account: monthly, strip price and weighted average strip. The process of creating and editing all of these types of alerts is similar, but there are important differences.

After selecting to create a new alert, enter a unique name for your alert. Once you select an energy market, the available alert types will be shown. For power energy markets, monthly and strip price alerts are available. For the natural gas energy market, monthly, strip price and weighted average strip alerts are available.

Monthly Alerts

When creating or editing a monthly energy market alert, a single delivery month is available to be selected. There are up to four years of delivery months available. Once you select a delivery month, the most recent market price for that delivery month is shown below.

Strip Price Alerts

A strip price is the average of a sequential series of monthly futures contracts, and can be a good indicator of where energy commodity prices are for those months.

Strip and Weighted Average Strip Alerts

A strip price is the average of a sequential series of monthly futures contracts, and can be a good indicator of where energy commodity prices are for those months. Weighted average strip pricing uses the same average of monthly futures contracts, but also allocates different weights to the various prices included.

Some customers may find these pricing averages helpful as a part of their risk management plan. My Account supports creating Energy Market Alerts for a single month, but not multiple months. In order to have equivalents to strip and weighted average strip pricing, users can create separate sequential alerts for each month in which you want to monitor pricing. The ability to create strip pricing alerts was available in previous versions of the portal, but will not be available in the new My Account.

The "weight" in the weighted average strip alert is the monthly volume.

Working With Your Active Energy Market Alerts

The Energy Market Alerts section in Preferences displays the alerts the user has created. Active alerts are those that have not reached the Delivery date (expiration of the specified NYMEX futures contract). Once the delivery date has been reached, an alert becomes expired. Expired Energy Market alerts are listed in the Expired Energy Market Alerts lists.

From the Active Energy Market alerts section, you can edit or delete any alert you have created.

Active Energy Market Alerts can be edited and deleted. Expired Energy Market alerts can be deleted, but not edited.

The Active Energy Market Alerts section displays the alerts you have created which have not reached their delivery date (expiration of the specified NYMEX futures contract). These active alerts will generate messages and emails when their thresholds are met. Once the delivery date has been reached, the "Expired Energy Market Alerts" section will be displayed.

Energy market alerts are individual to each user in My Account.  When an Energy Market Alert is set and if a market price exceeds the high set price or goes below the low set price, the alert messages will be sent to the single email address for the user who created the alert. If you have other members on your team who have access to your Direct Energy accounts, they will need to create their own alerts.

Editing Active Alerts

Any value of a Energy Market alert can be edited. After the changes are saved, the alert will use the updated information to generate alerts. An email and message will be sent to the user with the details of the edited values of the alert.

Any value of an alert can be edited. The same rules for ensuring valid values are entered are still enforced when editing an alert.

Energy Market Alert Emails

When a Energy Market Alert is set and if a market price exceeds the high set price or goes below the low set price, customers will receive an email from My Account.

When you create or edit a Energy Market alert, a message is sent to the email address for the user who created the Energy Market alert.

Energy Market Alert Message Center

When a Energy Market Alert is set and if a market price exceeds the high set price or goes below the low set price, My Account users will receive emails and messages in the Message Center. The My Account Message Center will display many different types of communications from Direct Energy Business, including energy market alert related messages.

When you create or edit a Energy Market alert, a message is sent to your My Account Message Center.

Working With Your Expired Energy Market Alerts

The Energy Market Alerts section in Preferences displays all of the alerts the user has created. Those alerts which have reached their delivery date (expiration of the specified market futures contract) are displayed in the Expired Alerts section. This section is collapsed by default. If there are no expired alerts to display, this section is not shown. Expired alerts will not generate messages and emails. Expired alerts can be deleted, but not edited.

Expired Energy Market alerts can be deleted, but not edited.
